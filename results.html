The Data Doesn't Lie: Time Lost to Manual Scheduling

99+ Hours Annually

The average administrator spends the equivalent of 12 full workdays every year just tracking and assigning substitute coverage.

The "Friday Rush": A 41.7% Spike in Work.

The manual burden is not consistent. Our survey shows that the time spent on sub coverage **spikes to 57.8 minutes on Fridays** compared to **40.8 minutes on Tuesdays**. This 41.7% surge in administrative work pulls you away from closing out the week with students and staff.

Paper is 2.85x Slower than Spreadsheets.

The method matters. Administrators using **Pen and Paper** spend **347 minutes** a week on scheduling, nearly double the time of "Combination" methods and **2.85 times** more than those using a "Spreadsheet". Automation is the only path to zero minutes spent.

The Middle School Time Sink.

When analyzing the burden by school level, **Middle School administrators reported the highest time waste**, averaging almost **349 minutes** per week. High School administrators, likely due to larger scale and more dedicated support staff, see the least time spent.
